## Cotonou / PHMD insectary — photo search notes (2026-07-21)

The “huge spider with 8 rearing greenhouses as legs” memory points to **IITA Plant Health Management Division / Biological Control Centre, Cotonou, Benin** (programme hub from **1988**), not only Ibadan.

### Confirmed in literature (no free building photo found)
- BCP moved to Cotonou, 1988; rearing also at Abeokuta / Ibadan earlier — [IAES/CGIAR impact brief](https://iaes.cgiar.org/sites/default/files/pdf/5.impact_major-pest.pdf)
- Screenhouse thermal research at **IITA BCP Cotonou**; mentions “Biological Control Centre for Africa… part of the Plant Health Management Division” and **Figure 1.1** of the centre — Acta Horticulturae / thesis: [doi:10.17660/actahortic.1997.443.4](https://doi.org/10.17660/actahortic.1997.443.4)
- Station overview: [iita.org/iita-countries/benin](https://www.iita.org/iita-countries/benin/)
- Insect collection feature (campus life, not spider plan): [CGIAR — West Africa’s largest insect collection](https://www.cgiar.org/news-events/news/introducing-west-africas-largest-insect-collection/)
- IITA media library (may contain unreleased campus shots; check license before reuse): [Flickr — iita-media-library](https://www.flickr.com/photos/iita-media-library/)

### Not suitable / not the spider
- Commons “Immeuble… quartier IITA Cotonou” is mis-described (bridge / construction) — not the insectary complex.
- No Wikimedia / CC aerial of the radial eight-greenhouse layout located in this pass.
